# GEOMETRY PROBLEMS - COMPREHENSIVE COLLECTION
# Geometry problems with solutions and detailed explanations

# BASIC SHAPES - PERIMETER
Problem: Find the perimeter of a square with side length 8 cm.
Solution: 32 cm
Explanation: Perimeter of square = 4 × side = 4 × 8 = 32 cm.

Problem: Find the perimeter of a rectangle with length 12 cm and width 7 cm.
Solution: 38 cm
Explanation: Perimeter of rectangle = 2(length + width) = 2(12 + 7) = 2(19) = 38 cm.

Problem: Find the perimeter of a triangle with sides 5 cm, 8 cm, and 11 cm.
Solution: 24 cm
Explanation: Perimeter of triangle = sum of all sides = 5 + 8 + 11 = 24 cm.

Problem: Find the perimeter of a regular hexagon with side length 4 cm.
Solution: 24 cm
Explanation: Regular hexagon has 6 equal sides. Perimeter = 6 × 4 = 24 cm.

# BASIC SHAPES - AREA
Problem: Find the area of a square with side length 9 m.
Solution: 81 m²
Explanation: Area of square = side² = 9² = 81 m².

Problem: Find the area of a rectangle with length 15 m and width 6 m.
Solution: 90 m²
Explanation: Area of rectangle = length × width = 15 × 6 = 90 m².

Problem: Find the area of a triangle with base 12 cm and height 8 cm.
Solution: 48 cm²
Explanation: Area of triangle = (1/2) × base × height = (1/2) × 12 × 8 = 48 cm².

Problem: Find the area of a parallelogram with base 10 cm and height 6 cm.
Solution: 60 cm²
Explanation: Area of parallelogram = base × height = 10 × 6 = 60 cm².

# CIRCLES - CIRCUMFERENCE
Problem: Find the circumference of a circle with radius 5 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 31.4 cm
Explanation: Circumference = 2πr = 2 × 3.14 × 5 = 31.4 cm.

Problem: Find the circumference of a circle with diameter 12 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 37.68 cm
Explanation: Circumference = πd = 3.14 × 12 = 37.68 cm.

Problem: Find the radius of a circle with circumference 31.4 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 5 cm
Explanation: C = 2πr, so r = C/(2π) = 31.4/(2 × 3.14) = 31.4/6.28 = 5 cm.

# CIRCLES - AREA
Problem: Find the area of a circle with radius 6 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 113.04 cm²
Explanation: Area = πr² = 3.14 × 6² = 3.14 × 36 = 113.04 cm².

Problem: Find the area of a circle with diameter 10 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 78.5 cm²
Explanation: Radius = diameter/2 = 10/2 = 5 cm. Area = πr² = 3.14 × 5² = 3.14 × 25 = 78.5 cm².

Problem: Find the radius of a circle with area 78.5 cm². (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 5 cm
Explanation: A = πr², so r² = A/π = 78.5/3.14 = 25. r = √25 = 5 cm.

# TRIANGLES - PYTHAGOREAN THEOREM
Problem: Find the hypotenuse of a right triangle with legs 6 cm and 8 cm.
Solution: 10 cm
Explanation: Using Pythagorean theorem: c² = a² + b² = 6² + 8² = 36 + 64 = 100. c = √100 = 10 cm.

Problem: Find the missing leg of a right triangle with hypotenuse 13 cm and one leg 5 cm.
Solution: 12 cm
Explanation: Using Pythagorean theorem: a² = c² - b² = 13² - 5² = 169 - 25 = 144. a = √144 = 12 cm.

Problem: Is a triangle with sides 7 cm, 24 cm, and 25 cm a right triangle?
Solution: Yes
Explanation: Check Pythagorean theorem: 7² + 24² = 49 + 576 = 625 = 25². Since a² + b² = c², it's a right triangle.

# TRIANGLES - TRIGONOMETRY
Problem: In a right triangle, if angle A = 30° and the hypotenuse is 10 cm, find the opposite side.
Solution: 5 cm
Explanation: sin(A) = opposite/hypotenuse. sin(30°) = 0.5. So opposite = 0.5 × 10 = 5 cm.

Problem: In a right triangle, if angle B = 45° and the adjacent side is 8 cm, find the hypotenuse.
Solution: 8√2 cm ≈ 11.31 cm
Explanation: cos(B) = adjacent/hypotenuse. cos(45°) = √2/2 ≈ 0.707. So hypotenuse = 8/0.707 ≈ 11.31 cm.

Problem: Find tan(60°) in a right triangle with opposite side 3√3 and adjacent side 3.
Solution: √3 ≈ 1.732
Explanation: tan(θ) = opposite/adjacent = (3√3)/3 = √3 ≈ 1.732.

# VOLUME - PRISMS
Problem: Find the volume of a rectangular prism with length 8 cm, width 5 cm, and height 4 cm.
Solution: 160 cm³
Explanation: Volume = length × width × height = 8 × 5 × 4 = 160 cm³.

Problem: Find the volume of a cube with side length 6 cm.
Solution: 216 cm³
Explanation: Volume of cube = side³ = 6³ = 216 cm³.

Problem: Find the volume of a triangular prism with base area 24 cm² and height 10 cm.
Solution: 240 cm³
Explanation: Volume = base area × height = 24 × 10 = 240 cm³.

# VOLUME - CYLINDERS
Problem: Find the volume of a cylinder with radius 4 cm and height 10 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 502.4 cm³
Explanation: Volume = πr²h = 3.14 × 4² × 10 = 3.14 × 16 × 10 = 502.4 cm³.

Problem: Find the height of a cylinder with volume 314 cm³ and radius 5 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 4 cm
Explanation: V = πr²h, so h = V/(πr²) = 314/(3.14 × 5²) = 314/(3.14 × 25) = 314/78.5 = 4 cm.

# VOLUME - SPHERES
Problem: Find the volume of a sphere with radius 6 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 904.32 cm³
Explanation: Volume = (4/3)πr³ = (4/3) × 3.14 × 6³ = (4/3) × 3.14 × 216 = 904.32 cm³.

Problem: Find the radius of a sphere with volume 113.04 cm³. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 3 cm
Explanation: V = (4/3)πr³, so r³ = (3V)/(4π) = (3 × 113.04)/(4 × 3.14) = 339.12/12.56 = 27. r = ∛27 = 3 cm.

# SURFACE AREA
Problem: Find the surface area of a cube with side length 5 cm.
Solution: 150 cm²
Explanation: Surface area of cube = 6 × side² = 6 × 5² = 6 × 25 = 150 cm².

Problem: Find the surface area of a rectangular prism with dimensions 4 cm × 6 cm × 8 cm.
Solution: 208 cm²
Explanation: Surface area = 2(lw + lh + wh) = 2(4×6 + 4×8 + 6×8) = 2(24 + 32 + 48) = 2(104) = 208 cm².

Problem: Find the surface area of a cylinder with radius 3 cm and height 8 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 207.24 cm²
Explanation: Surface area = 2πr² + 2πrh = 2 × 3.14 × 3² + 2 × 3.14 × 3 × 8 = 56.52 + 150.72 = 207.24 cm².

# COMPOSITE SHAPES
Problem: Find the area of a shape consisting of a rectangle 10 cm × 6 cm with a semicircle of radius 3 cm attached to one side.
Solution: 78.27 cm²
Explanation: Rectangle area = 10 × 6 = 60 cm². Semicircle area = (1/2)πr² = (1/2) × 3.14 × 3² = 14.13 cm². Total area = 60 + 14.13 = 74.13 cm².

# SIMILAR TRIANGLES
Problem: Two similar triangles have corresponding sides in ratio 2:3. If the smaller triangle has area 20 cm², what is the area of the larger triangle?
Solution: 45 cm²
Explanation: Area ratio = (side ratio)² = (2/3)² = 4/9. So larger area = 20 × (9/4) = 45 cm².

# COORDINATE GEOMETRY
Problem: Find the distance between points (3, 4) and (7, 1).
Solution: 5 units
Explanation: Distance = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²] = √[(7 - 3)² + (1 - 4)²] = √[4² + (-3)²] = √[16 + 9] = √25 = 5.

Problem: Find the midpoint of the segment joining points (2, 8) and (10, 4).
Solution: (6, 6)
Explanation: Midpoint = ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2) = ((2 + 10)/2, (8 + 4)/2) = (12/2, 12/2) = (6, 6).

Problem: Find the slope of the line passing through points (1, 2) and (5, 10).
Solution: 2
Explanation: Slope = (y₂ - y₁)/(x₂ - x₁) = (10 - 2)/(5 - 1) = 8/4 = 2.

# ANGLES
Problem: Find the measure of angle x if it's supplementary to angle 125°.
Solution: 55°
Explanation: Supplementary angles add to 180°. So x = 180° - 125° = 55°.

Problem: Find the measure of angle y if it's complementary to angle 67°.
Solution: 23°
Explanation: Complementary angles add to 90°. So y = 90° - 67° = 23°.

Problem: In a triangle, two angles measure 45° and 65°. Find the third angle.
Solution: 70°
Explanation: Triangle angles sum to 180°. Third angle = 180° - 45° - 65° = 70°.

# PARALLEL LINES AND TRANSVERSALS
Problem: If two parallel lines are cut by a transversal and one angle measures 110°, find the measure of its corresponding angle.
Solution: 110°
Explanation: Corresponding angles are equal when lines are parallel.

Problem: If two parallel lines are cut by a transversal and one angle measures 70°, find the measure of its alternate interior angle.
Solution: 70°
Explanation: Alternate interior angles are equal when lines are parallel.

# POLYGONS
Problem: Find the sum of interior angles of a hexagon.
Solution: 720°
Explanation: Sum of interior angles = (n - 2) × 180° = (6 - 2) × 180° = 4 × 180° = 720°.

Problem: Find the measure of each interior angle of a regular octagon.
Solution: 135°
Explanation: Each interior angle = (n - 2) × 180° ÷ n = (8 - 2) × 180° ÷ 8 = 6 × 180° ÷ 8 = 1080° ÷ 8 = 135°.

# CIRCLES - ARCS AND SECTORS
Problem: Find the length of an arc with central angle 60° in a circle with radius 12 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 12.56 cm
Explanation: Arc length = (θ/360°) × 2πr = (60/360) × 2 × 3.14 × 12 = (1/6) × 75.36 = 12.56 cm.

Problem: Find the area of a sector with central angle 90° in a circle with radius 8 cm. (Use π ≈ 3.14)
Solution: 50.24 cm²
Explanation: Sector area = (θ/360°) × πr² = (90/360) × 3.14 × 8² = (1/4) × 3.14 × 64 = 50.24 cm².

# 3D GEOMETRY - NETS
Problem: How many faces does a cube have?
Solution: 6 faces
Explanation: A cube has 6 square faces: top, bottom, front, back, left, right.

Problem: How many edges does a triangular prism have?
Solution: 9 edges
Explanation: A triangular prism has 3 edges on each triangular base (6 total) plus 3 edges connecting the bases (3 total) = 9 edges.

# TRANSFORMATIONS
Problem: A point (3, 4) is reflected over the x-axis. What are its new coordinates?
Solution: (3, -4)
Explanation: Reflection over x-axis changes the sign of the y-coordinate while keeping x-coordinate the same.

Problem: A point (2, 5) is translated 3 units right and 2 units down. What are its new coordinates?
Solution: (5, 3)
Explanation: Translation: (x + 3, y - 2) = (2 + 3, 5 - 2) = (5, 3).

# CIRCLES - CHORDS AND TANGENTS
Problem: A circle has radius 10 cm. Find the length of a chord that is 6 cm from the center.
Solution: 16 cm
Explanation: Draw perpendicular from center to chord, creating right triangle with legs 6 cm and half-chord length. Using Pythagorean theorem: (half-chord)² + 6² = 10². (half-chord)² = 100 - 36 = 64. half-chord = 8 cm. Full chord = 16 cm.

# REGULAR POLYGONS
Problem: Find the area of a regular pentagon with side length 8 cm. (Use formula: A = (1/4)√(25 + 10√5)s²)
Solution: 110.11 cm²
Explanation: A = (1/4)√(25 + 10√5) × 8² = (1/4)√(25 + 10√5) × 64 = 16√(25 + 10√5) ≈ 16√(25 + 22.36) = 16√47.36 ≈ 16 × 6.88 = 110.11 cm².

# GEOMETRIC PROBABILITY
Problem: A dart is thrown at a square target with side length 20 cm. The bullseye is a circle with radius 5 cm at the center. What is the probability of hitting the bullseye?
Solution: π/16 ≈ 0.196 or 19.6%
Explanation: Area of square = 20² = 400 cm². Area of circle = π × 5² = 25π cm². Probability = 25π/400 = π/16 ≈ 0.196.

# GEOMETRIC SEQUENCES
Problem: In a geometric sequence, the first term is 3 and the common ratio is 2. Find the 5th term.
Solution: 48
Explanation: a₅ = a₁ × r^(n-1) = 3 × 2^(5-1) = 3 × 2⁴ = 3 × 16 = 48.

# GEOMETRIC MEAN
Problem: Find the geometric mean of 4 and 9.
Solution: 6
Explanation: Geometric mean = √(4 × 9) = √36 = 6.

# GOLDEN RATIO
Problem: A rectangle has length 10 cm and width 6.18 cm. What is the ratio of length to width?
Solution: Approximately 1.618 (golden ratio)
Explanation: Ratio = 10/6.18 ≈ 1.618, which is the golden ratio φ.

# TESSELLATION
Problem: Which regular polygons can tessellate a plane by themselves?
Solution: Equilateral triangles, squares, and regular hexagons
Explanation: These shapes have interior angles that divide evenly into 360°, allowing them to fit together without gaps.

# GEOMETRIC CONSTRUCTIONS
Problem: Construct an equilateral triangle with side length 6 cm. What is the height?
Solution: 3√3 cm ≈ 5.20 cm
Explanation: In an equilateral triangle, height = (√3/2) × side = (√3/2) × 6 = 3√3 cm.

# SPHERICAL GEOMETRY
Problem: On a sphere, the sum of angles in a triangle with vertices at the North Pole and two points on the equator 90° apart is 270°. What is the spherical excess?
Solution: 90°
Explanation: Spherical excess = sum of angles - 180° = 270° - 180° = 90°.

# NON-EUCLIDEAN GEOMETRY
Problem: In hyperbolic geometry, the sum of angles in a triangle is always less than 180°. If a triangle has angles 50°, 60°, and 65°, what is the angle defect?
Solution: 5°
Explanation: Angle defect = 180° - (50° + 60° + 65°) = 180° - 175° = 5°.

# PROJECTIVE GEOMETRY
Problem: In projective geometry, parallel lines meet at a point at infinity. How many points at infinity are there in a plane?
Solution: One for each direction (infinitely many)
Explanation: Each set of parallel lines has its own point at infinity, and there are infinitely many directions.

# FRACTAL GEOMETRY
Problem: A Koch snowflake starts with an equilateral triangle of side length 9 cm. After one iteration, what is the perimeter?
Solution: 48 cm
Explanation: Each side is divided into 3 parts and middle part replaced with 2 sides of equilateral triangle. New perimeter = 4 × original perimeter = 4 × 12 = 48 cm.

# TOPOLOGY
Problem: A coffee cup and a donut are topologically equivalent. What topological property do they share?
Solution: They both have one hole (genus 1)
Explanation: Both surfaces have exactly one hole, making them topologically equivalent.
